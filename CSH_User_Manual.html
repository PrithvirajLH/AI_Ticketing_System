1 Getting Started

1.1 — Logging In

The CSNHC Ticketing System uses Microsoft Azure Active Directory (SSO) for authentication. To sign in:

  1. Navigate to the ticketing system URL provided by your IT department.
  2. Click "Sign in with Microsoft".
  3. Enter your organizational Microsoft credentials (email and password).
  4. Complete any multi-factor authentication (MFA) if prompted.
  5. You will be redirected to your personalized dashboard.
First-Time Users On your first login, the system automatically creates your profile from your Microsoft Graph data (display name, email, department, location). Your default role is Employee.

1.2 — Understanding Your Role

Your role determines what you can see and do in the system. There are 5 roles, listed from least to most access:

RoleWhoSummary
EMPLOYEE All staff members Create tickets, track your own requests, add messages and attachments to your tickets.
AGENT Support team members Everything Employee can do, plus: view and work on team tickets, assign tickets to yourself, use canned responses, bulk operations.
LEAD Team leads / supervisors Everything Agent can do, plus: Triage board, reports & analytics, team management, SLA monitoring, manager views.
TEAM ADMIN Department administrators Everything Lead can do, plus: configure routing rules, automation rules, custom fields, audit logs. Scoped to their assigned team.
OWNER Platform administrators Unrestricted access across all teams. Can manage categories, assign roles, configure all system settings.

4 Creating Tickets

EMPLOYEE AGENT LEAD TEAM ADMIN OWNER

All users can create tickets. There are two ways:

Method 1: New Ticket Page

  1. Click "+ New Ticket" in the sidebar or top navigation.
  2. Fill in the Subject (required, max 200 characters) — a brief summary of the issue.
  3. Fill in the Description (required) — detailed explanation. Supports rich text formatting.
  4. Select Priority: P1 (Critical), P2 (High), P3 (Medium, default), P4 (Low).
  5. Select the Team/Department the request should be routed to.
  6. Optionally select a Category to classify the ticket.
  7. Fill in any Custom Fields that appear (these vary by team/category).
  8. The Facility field auto-populates from your Microsoft profile (office location).
  9. Click "Create Ticket".

Method 2: Command Palette

  1. Press Ctrl+K (Windows) or Cmd+K (Mac) to open the command palette.
  2. Select "Create Ticket" from the options.
  3. A modal dialog opens with the same form fields.
Automatic Routing If routing rules are configured, your ticket may be automatically assigned to a specific team and/or agent based on keywords in the subject and description.

After Ticket Creation

  • A unique Ticket ID (e.g., CSH-1234) is assigned automatically.
  • The ticket starts in NEW status.
  • SLA timers begin (first response due, resolution due).
  • If round-robin assignment is enabled on the team, an agent is auto-assigned.
  • You are automatically added as a follower of the ticket.
  • You will receive notifications when the ticket is updated.

7 Ticket Workflow & Status Transitions

Tickets follow a defined workflow. Not all status changes are possible — only valid transitions are shown in the dropdown.

Status Definitions

StatusMeaning
NEWJust created, not yet reviewed by support team
TRIAGEDReviewed and categorized, awaiting assignment
ASSIGNEDAssigned to a specific agent
IN_PROGRESSAgent is actively working on the issue
WAITING_ON_REQUESTERWaiting for the requester to provide information
WAITING_ON_VENDORWaiting for an external vendor/third party
RESOLVEDIssue has been fixed/addressed
CLOSEDTicket is finalized (no further action)
REOPENEDPreviously resolved ticket has been reopened

Typical Workflow

NEW → TRIAGED → ASSIGNED → IN_PROGRESS → RESOLVED → CLOSED
                   ↓                ↓
        WAITING_ON_REQUESTER    REOPENED → (back to IN_PROGRESS)
        WAITING_ON_VENDOR

Automatic Status Changes

  • When a ticket is assigned and its status is NEW, TRIAGED, or REOPENED, it automatically changes to ASSIGNED.
  • When a ticket is transferred to a new team without an assignee, ASSIGNED/IN_PROGRESS status reverts to the appropriate earlier state.
  • SLA timers pause during WAITING_ON_REQUESTER and WAITING_ON_VENDOR statuses.

9.1 — Understanding SLA

SLAs define response and resolution time targets for tickets based on priority:

SLA MetricWhat It Measures
First Response TimeTime from ticket creation until the first agent response
Resolution TimeTime from ticket creation until the ticket is resolved

SLA Visual Indicators

ColorStatusMeaning

AmberCaution25-50% of time remaining
OrangeAt RiskLess than 25% of time remaining
RedBreachedTime has expired — SLA violated
GrayPausedSLA timer is paused (waiting on requester/vendor)

9.2 — SLA Configuration

TEAM ADMIN OWNER

Navigate to Admin → SLA Policies to configure:

  • Per-priority targets — Set different first response and resolution hours for P1, P2, P3, P4.
  • Business hours — Configure working days, start/end times, and timezone. SLA timers only count business hours.
  • Holidays — Add holidays when SLA timers should pause.
  • Team assignment — Assign policies to specific teams.
  • Escalation — Enable automatic notifications when SLA is at risk (configurable threshold, default 80%).
  • Breach notification roles — Choose who gets notified on breach (Agent, Lead, Manager, Owner).

14 Admin: Routing Rules

TEAM ADMIN OWNER

Routing rules automatically assign incoming tickets to the right team based on keywords. Navigate to Admin → Routing Rules.

How Routing Works

  1. When a ticket is created, the system scans the subject and description for keyword matches.
  2. Rules are evaluated in priority order (lower number = higher priority).
  3. The first matching rule wins and routes the ticket to its configured team.
  4. If a specific assignee is set on the rule, the ticket is also assigned to that person.
  5. If no rule matches, the ticket goes to whatever team was selected by the requester.

Creating a Routing Rule

  1. Click "Create Rule".
  2. Enter a Name (e.g., "Password Reset Requests").
  3. Add Keywords (e.g., "password", "reset", "locked out"). These are matched case-insensitively.
  4. Select the Target Team to route matching tickets to.
  5. Optionally select a specific Assignee in the team.
  6. Set the Priority (execution order, lower = first).
  7. Toggle Active/Inactive.
  8. Click "Save".
Team Admin Scope Team Admins can only create and manage routing rules for their own primary team. Owners can manage rules for any team.

21 Email Integration

The system supports ticket creation and updates via email.

Creating Tickets via Email

  • Send an email to the configured support address.
  • The subject becomes the ticket subject.
  • The body becomes the ticket description.
  • The sender's email is matched to an existing user (or a profile is created).

Email Threading

  • Replies to ticket notification emails are automatically threaded as new messages on the original ticket.
  • Each ticket has a unique reply token embedded in notification emails.
  • The system matches inbound emails by message ID headers and reply tokens.

Email Channel

Tickets created via email are marked with the EMAIL channel. Tickets created through the web portal are marked PORTAL.

22 Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I can't see any tickets. What's wrong?

A: As an Employee, you only see tickets you created. If you're an Agent and see no tickets, your team may not have any assigned tickets. Contact your team lead to verify your team membership.

Q: How do I change my role?

A: Only Owners can change user roles. Contact your platform administrator.

Q: Can I be a member of multiple teams?

A: Yes. You can be added as a member (Agent, Lead, or Admin) to multiple teams. Your ticket visibility includes tickets from all your teams.

Q: What happens to my tickets if I'm removed from a team?

A: Tickets assigned to you remain assigned. Your team lead or admin should reassign them. You will lose visibility to team tickets you didn't create or aren't assigned to.

Q: How does Round Robin assignment work?

A: When a ticket arrives in a team with Round Robin enabled, the system assigns it to the next team member in rotation. The system tracks who was last assigned and moves to the next person. If the assignee is unavailable, it moves to the next member.

Q: Can I reopen a closed ticket?

A: Yes, resolved tickets can be reopened (status changes to REOPENED). Check the status dropdown for available transitions from the current status.

Q: Does the SLA timer run during weekends?

A: If the SLA policy has "Business Hours Only" enabled (which is the default), the timer only counts business hours as configured. Weekends and holidays are excluded.

Q: How do I get notified about SLA breaches?

A: You'll receive in-app notifications. If you're a follower of the ticket, you'll also receive email notifications. SLA escalation notifications are sent to the roles configured in the SLA policy (default: Agent and Lead).

Q: What's the difference between "Transfer" and "Assign"?

A: Assign changes which agent within the same team handles the ticket. Transfer moves the ticket to a completely different team (and optionally assigns to someone in that new team).

Q: Can I undo a status change or assignment?

A: There is no "undo" button, but you can change the status again or reassign to the previous person. All changes are logged in the ticket timeline for audit purposes.

Q: How do internal notes differ from public messages?

A: Public messages are visible to everyone, including the Employee/requester. Internal notes are visible only to Agents, Leads, Admins, and Owners. Use internal notes for team discussions about the ticket that the requester shouldn't see.

Q: What file types and sizes can I upload?

A: Most common file types are supported. The maximum file size is configured by your administrator (default: 10 MB per file). Uploaded files are automatically scanned for malware.

Q: I'm a Team Admin but I can't see other teams' data. Is this correct?

A: Yes. Team Admins are scoped to their primary team only. This is by design for organizational security. Only the Owner role has cross-team visibility. Contact your Owner if you need broader access.
